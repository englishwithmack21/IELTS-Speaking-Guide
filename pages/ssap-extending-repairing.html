Part One

Warm-Up Discussion

Activating Ideas and Experiences

Purpose: This section helps you activate your background knowledge about travel and tourism. It also allows you to begin speaking naturally before learning the strategies for extending answers.

In IELTS Speaking, many questions are based on everyday topics such as travel, hobbies, and daily life. These questions are designed to encourage you to speak comfortably and express personal experiences. Before learning new speaking strategies, it is useful to explore the topic through discussion.
ACTIVITY 1 General Discussion Questions

Answer the following questions about travel. Try to speak naturally and explain your ideas.

  1. Do you enjoy travelling? Why or why not?
  2. What kind of places do you usually like to visit when you travel?
  3. Do you prefer travelling alone or with other people?
  4. What is the most interesting place you have ever visited?
  5. How often do you travel each year?
  6. What is one country or city you would like to visit in the future?
  7. Do you prefer visiting famous tourist attractions or less crowded places?
  8. What do you usually do when you travel to a new place?
  9. Do you think travelling is important for personal development?
  10. What is one memorable experience you had while travelling?
ACTIVITY 2 Follow-Up Questions

Choose three questions from Activity 1 and try to develop your answers further. Ask yourself the following questions to help you extend your response:

  • Why?
  • Can you give an example?
  • How did you feel about that experience?
  • What did you learn from it?
Example

Question: Do you enjoy travelling?

Short answer: Yes, I do.

Extended answer: Yes, I really enjoy travelling because it allows me to experience different cultures. For example, last year I visited a coastal town where I tried several local dishes and explored the old city centre. It was a very relaxing experience and it made me realise how enjoyable travelling can be.

Write one or two reasons for your answer.
ACTIVITY 4 Extending Your Answer

Choose three questions from Activity 1 and try to extend your answers. Use the following prompts to help you:

  • Answer the question clearly.
  • Explain your reason.
  • Give an example.
  • Describe how you felt about the experience.

Part Three — Speaking Strategy

The Layering Method

Developing answers step by step

Purpose: In many speaking situations, learners give answers that are too short or repetitive. One of the most effective ways to improve speaking performance is to develop answers logically and clearly. The Layering Method helps you expand your answers step by step. Instead of repeating the same idea, you add new information that supports your opinion. This method is particularly useful in speaking tests because it helps you produce longer, clearer responses.

The Layering Method — Framework

L1
Statement
Answer the question clearly and directly.
L2
Reason
Explain why you think that or why you have that preference.
L3
Example
Give a real experience or specific situation to support your idea.
L4
Feeling
Describe your personal reaction or emotional response.
L5
Reflection
Explain what the experience taught you or why it was meaningful.
▶ Full Example — Layered Answer
Question: Do you enjoy travelling?
Statement
Yes, I really enjoy travelling.
Reason
I like travelling because it allows me to experience different cultures.
Example
For example, last year I visited Kyoto in Japan and spent several days exploring traditional temples and trying local food.
Feeling
I found the experience incredibly exciting and memorable.
Reflection
Trips like that make me appreciate how diverse the world can be.

Part Four — Speaking Strategy

The 4-Step Answer Engine

Structuring Clear and Natural Responses

Purpose: While the Layering Method helps you extend your answers, many learners still struggle with organising their ideas quickly during conversation. When you are asked a question, it is useful to have a simple structure that helps you respond immediately. The 4-Step Answer Engine is a practical framework that helps speakers develop answers logically and naturally. Instead of giving short or repetitive responses, you can follow four simple steps to organise your ideas.

The 4-Step Answer Engine — Framework

S1
Direct Answer
Give a clear and direct response to the question. Don't delay — answer first.
S2
Reason
Explain why you think that or why you have that preference.
S3
Example
Provide a real example or personal experience to support your idea.
S4
Reflection
Add a final comment about your feelings, thoughts, or what you learned.
▶ Full Example — 4-Step Answer
Question: Do you enjoy travelling?
Direct Answer
Yes, I really enjoy travelling.
Reason
I enjoy it because it allows me to experience new cultures and environments.
Example
For instance, last year I visited Kyoto in Japan where I explored several traditional temples and tried different kinds of local food.
Reflection
Experiences like that make travelling very meaningful and memorable for me.

Part Six

Natural Spoken Language

Using Idioms and Phrasal Verbs in Conversation

Purpose: In natural conversation, speakers often use idiomatic expressions and phrasal verbs instead of overly formal vocabulary. These expressions make spoken language sound more natural and conversational. In speaking tests and everyday communication, using natural expressions appropriately can help your answers sound more fluent and engaging.

Section 1 — Common Travel Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal verbs are combinations of verbs and particles (such as up, out, or off) that create new meanings. The following phrasal verbs are commonly used when talking about travel.

Phrasal Verb Meaning Example Sentence
set off begin a journey We set off early in the morning to avoid traffic.
check out visit or examine a place We checked out several museums during our trip.
get away leave your usual environment to relax I like to get away from the city during holidays.
head back return to a place After dinner, we headed back to our hotel.
look around explore a place We spent the afternoon looking around the old town.
run into meet unexpectedly I ran into an old friend while travelling in Seoul.

Section 2 — Idiomatic Expressions for Describing Travel

Idiomatic expressions are phrases whose meanings cannot be understood from the individual words alone. These expressions are common in spoken English.

hidden gem
A wonderful place that few people know about
"The village turned out to be a hidden gem."
off the beaten path
Away from common tourist routes
"We decided to visit a town that was off the beaten path."
a once-in-a-lifetime experience
A very rare and memorable event
"Seeing the northern lights was a once-in-a-lifetime experience."
soak up the atmosphere
Enjoy the feeling or environment of a place
"We spent the evening soaking up the atmosphere of the city."
tourist hotspot
A place that attracts many tourists
"Paris is a major tourist hotspot in Europe."

Part Eight

Expanding Short Answers

From 15 Seconds to 60 Seconds

Purpose: Many learners answer questions very quickly and stop speaking after only a few seconds. However, effective speaking requires you to develop your ideas clearly and provide enough detail for the listener. In this section, you will practise expanding short answers into longer responses — moving from a brief response (about 10–15 seconds) to a more developed answer (about 45–60 seconds). Use the strategies you learned earlier: The Layering Method and The 4-Step Answer Engine.

Section 1 — Understanding Answer Length

Do you enjoy travelling?
Short Answer — approx. 10–15 seconds
Yes, I enjoy travelling because it allows me to relax and visit interesting places.
Extended Answer — approx. 45–60 seconds
